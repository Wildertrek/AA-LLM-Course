# SPAR (Supervised Program for Alignment Research)

## Overview
**Mission:** A project-based program for people wanting to gain their first experience in alignment research through mentorship.
**URL:** [https://sparai.org/](https://sparai.org/)
**Location:** Remote-first.
**Duration:** 3-month research projects (Part-time, 5-20 hours/week).

## Project Categories & Examples (Spring 2026)
SPAR Spring 2026 is the largest cycle to date, offering over 130 projects. Below are the most relevant tracks for your work:

### 1. Agentic AI & Autonomous Systems
*   **Mapping the Belief-Action Gap in AI Agents** (Mentors: Mario Giulianelli, Raghu Arghal): Developing formal methods to measure deceptive alignment in goal-directed behavior.
*   **Agentic Situational Awareness** (Mentors: Diogo Cruz, Vamshi Krishna Bonagiri): Developing benchmarks for "Operational Situational Awareness" in agents.
*   **Self-Led LLM Agents** (Mentor: Samuel Brown): Investigating how autonomous agents behave when their goals evolve over time.
*   **Automating Circuit Interpretability with Agents** (Mentor: Georg Lange): Building agents that can automatically explain internal model features.

### 2. Model Psychology & Behavior
*   **What Conception of "Self" do Models Have?** (Mentor: Alexander Meinke): Quantifying how models identify with context vs. weights and how RL affects this.
*   **Monitoring and Attributing Implicit Personalization** (Mentor: Gabriele Sarti): Investigating how models form implicit beliefs about users (Sycophancy/Bias).
*   **Human Behavioral Phenomena in Language Models** (Mentor: Emilio Barkett): Adapting social science experimental designs to test for emergent human-like behaviors.
*   **Structural Fingerprinting** (Mentor: Evan Harris): Distinguishing between stable internal goals and simple stylistic mimicry.

### 3. Evaluations & Benchmarking
*   **Shutdown-Bench: Evaluating Shutdownability** (Mentors: Christos Ziakas, Elliott Thornley): Designing prompt-based tests for self-preserving behaviors in models.
*   **Benchmarking In-Context Intent Inference** (Mentor: Dylan Hadfield-Menell): Measuring how well models infer user goals from multi-turn interactions.
*   **Manta: Nonhuman Welfare Reasoning** (Mentor: Allen Lu): Evaluating how models reason about ethical subjects like animal welfare.

### 4. Mechanistic Interpretability
*   **Context-Induced Belief Geometry** (Mentor: Xavier Poncini): Analyzing "belief distributions" over hidden world states using linear probes.
*   **Neural Circuit Breakers** (Mentors: Dawn Song, Yiyou Sun): Mapping "misalignment vectors" to detect and halt harmful agent intent.

## Pedagogy: Learning Path for SPAR
Since SPAR is entry-level but research-focused, the pedagogy focuses on project execution and technical skills.

### Module 1: Pre-Requisite Knowledge
*   **Goal:** Reach a baseline understanding of AI safety.
*   **Requirement:** Complete an "AI Safety Fundamentals" course (e.g., from BlueDot Impact) or a student-led DeCal course if affiliated with a university.
*   **Action:** Familiarize yourself with the [Alignment Forum](https://www.alignmentforum.org/) top posts.

### Module 2: Skill Acquisition
*   **Technical:** Proficiency in Python, JAX/PyTorch, and Hugging Face Transformers.
*   **Policy:** Understanding of current AI regulations (EU AI Act, Executive Orders).

### Module 3: Project Selection & Mentorship Preparation
*   **Goal:** Select a project that matches your background.
*   **Action:** Review the list of available projects during the application cycle. 
*   **Preparation:** Draft a 1-page research proposal for a specific problem in alignment that interests you.

### Module 4: Output and Publication
*   **Goal:** Produce a tangible artifact.
*   **Focus:** Writing clearly for a technical audience. 
*   **Deliverable:** Aim for a preprint on arXiv or a high-quality post on the Alignment Forum.
