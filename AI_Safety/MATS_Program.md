# MATS (ML Alignment & Theory Scholars) Program

## Overview
**Mission:** To find and train talented individuals to reduce risks from unaligned artificial intelligence (AI).
**URL:** [https://www.matsprogram.org/](https://www.matsprogram.org/)
**Location:** Berkeley, CA and London, UK (In-person).
**Duration:** 12-week intensive research fellowship + possible 6-12 month extension.

## Focus Areas (Research Tracks)

### 1. Mechanistic Interpretability
*   **Focus:** Reverse-engineering the internal components of neural networks to understand how they represent concepts and perform computations.
*   **Key Projects:** Sparse Autoencoders for feature discovery, Representation Engineering (RepE), and circuit analysis (Transcoders).
*   **Relevance to Model Psychology:** This is the technical foundation for understanding the "internal mind" of the model.

### 2. Scalable Oversight & Alignment
*   **Focus:** Developing techniques to align models even when human evaluation is difficult or biased.
*   **Key Projects:** Addressing **Sycophancy**, the "Reversal Curse" in knowledge representation, and AI-assisted debate.
*   **Relevance to MindBench:** Investigating how models mirror or manipulate user beliefs is critical for personality-integrated agents.

### 3. Dangerous Capability Evaluations (Evaluations)
*   **Focus:** Proactively identifying and measuring hazardous capabilities before deployment.
*   **Key Projects:** The **WMDP Benchmark** (biosecurity/cyber), the **MACHIAVELLI Benchmark** for ethical trade-offs, and measuring **Situational Awareness**.
*   **Relevance:** This is the most direct path for your "Experience" gap—creating or refining benchmarks for agent safety.

### 4. Agent Foundations
*   **Focus:** Theoretical and empirical research into the fundamental nature of AI agents, decision theory, and multi-agent safety.
*   **Key Projects:** Blockchain exploit analysis, agentic unlearning, and formalizing agency.
*   **Relevance to MindBench:** This track is the natural home for your work on agent architectures and behavior.

### 5. Safeguards & Adversarial Robustness
*   **Focus:** Building defenses against jailbreaks, backdoors, and emergent misalignment.
*   **Key Projects:** Latent Adversarial Training (LAT) and investigating how fine-tuning can undo safety training.

## Pedagogy: Learning Path for MATS
To effectively learn what MATS offers and prepare for the program, follow this structured path:

### Module 1: Foundations of AI Safety
*   **Goal:** Understand the core arguments for AI risk and the landscape of alignment.
*   **Resources:** 
    *   Read the [AI Safety Fundamentals](https://www.aisafetyfundamentals.com/) curriculum (Alignment track).
    *   Study "The Case for AI Alignment" (various sources like LessWrong).
    *   Review the MATS [Scholar Research](https://www.matsprogram.org/scholars) to see previous outputs.

### Module 2: Technical Deep Dive (Choose a Track)
*   **Empirical:** Master PyTorch/Transformer Lens. Work through [BlueDot Impact's AISF curriculum](https://bluedot.impact.site/).
*   **Theory:** Study "Agent Foundations" (MIRI research) and singular learning theory.
*   **Governance:** Read "The Governance of AI" (Center for the Governance of AI).

### Module 3: Research Agenda Alignment
*   **Goal:** Identify a mentor and their specific research agenda.
*   **Action:** Read the blog posts and papers of current MATS mentors (e.g., Neel Nanda, Evan Hubinger).
*   **Practice:** Reproduce a small result from a recent mechanistic interpretability paper.

### Module 4: Artifact Creation
*   **Goal:** Produce a "proof of work" artifact.
*   **Action:** Write a blog post on LessWrong or Alignment Forum explaining a complex safety concept or a small experiment.
